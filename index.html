Dr. Norman borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexxico on how increase wheat yield - part of his life-long war o hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914- Born in Cresco, Iowa.

  • 1933- Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration".

  • 1935- Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed.
